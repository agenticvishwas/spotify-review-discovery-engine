# 1. Executive Summary

## Background

Spotify's long-term success depends on helping users continually discover meaningful new music rather than repeatedly consuming the same content.

While Spotify already provides recommendation systems such as Discover Weekly, Daily Mixes, Radio, AI DJ, Smart Shuffle, and Home Feed recommendations, user feedback across public platforms indicates that many listeners still struggle to discover music that feels genuinely new, relevant, and surprising.

Product teams currently receive valuable feedback through multiple public channels:

* Apple App Store Reviews
* Google Play Store Reviews
* Spotify Community Forums
* Reddit Discussions
* Social Media Conversations
* Feature Requests
* User Complaints

This feedback represents one of the richest sources of customer insight available to the organization.

However, the sheer volume of information makes manual analysis impractical.

Traditional approaches—reading reviews individually, keyword searching, or producing simple sentiment summaries—fail to answer strategic product questions such as:

* Why do users struggle to discover new music?
* Why do recommendation systems become repetitive?
* Which users experience the greatest discovery friction?
* What unmet discovery needs remain unsolved?
* Which product opportunities have the greatest potential impact?

This project addresses that challenge by building an AI-powered Review Discovery Engine capable of transforming large-scale unstructured user feedback into structured, evidence-backed product intelligence.

Rather than acting as a review summarizer, the system should function as an internal Voice of Customer platform that continuously converts raw user feedback into actionable insights for Product Managers.

---

# 2. Product Vision

## Vision Statement

Create an intelligent research platform that enables any Product Manager to understand customer problems in minutes instead of days by automatically transforming user feedback into structured product knowledge.

The system should answer not only **what users are saying**, but also:

* Why they are experiencing problems.
* What they are trying to accomplish.
* Which user segments are affected.
* How severe each problem is.
* What unmet needs consistently emerge.
* Which opportunities deserve prioritization.

The long-term objective is to make customer feedback continuously searchable, explainable, measurable, and actionable.

---

# 3. Product Mission

The mission of this project is to build an AI system that converts noisy, unstructured customer conversations into structured product intelligence that supports evidence-based decision making.

Success is measured not by the number of reviews processed, but by the quality, consistency, and usefulness of the insights produced.

Every insight generated by the system must remain traceable to the original customer evidence.

# 4. Business Objective

Assume the role of a Product Manager within Spotify's Growth organization.

One of the Growth team's strategic objectives is:

> Increase meaningful music discovery while reducing repetitive listening behavior.

Before proposing product improvements, experiments, or recommendation algorithm changes, the team must first understand user problems at scale.

This project represents the foundational research platform required to achieve that understanding.

The output of this system will directly support:

* Product strategy
* Growth experimentation
* Feature prioritization
* Recommendation quality improvements
* User segmentation
* Opportunity identification
* Executive reporting

---

# 5. Problem Statement

Current customer feedback is fragmented across multiple public platforms.

Although the volume of available feedback is extremely high, extracting meaningful insights remains expensive, inconsistent, and largely manual.

Existing approaches suffer from several limitations:

* Reviews are analyzed individually rather than collectively.
* Sentiment analysis lacks contextual understanding.
* Similar complaints are not clustered consistently.
* Root causes remain hidden beneath surface-level feedback.
* Jobs To Be Done are rarely inferred.
* User intent is difficult to quantify.
* Product opportunities are not automatically surfaced.
* Product managers spend excessive time synthesizing evidence.

As a result, valuable customer signals remain buried inside thousands of independent conversations.

The organization requires an AI-powered system capable of continuously converting these conversations into structured knowledge.

# 6. Guiding Principles

The following principles should guide every architectural and implementation decision.

### Principle 1 — Evidence Over Opinion

Every insight must be supported by underlying customer feedback.

The system should never produce unsupported conclusions.

---

### Principle 2 — Structure Before Summaries

The primary output is structured knowledge.

Narrative summaries are secondary artifacts generated from structured data.

---

### Principle 3 — Explainability

Every recommendation, opportunity, or conclusion should remain traceable to the customer evidence that produced it.

A Product Manager should always be able to inspect:

* supporting reviews
* confidence level
* reasoning
* contributing themes

---

### Principle 4 — Human-in-the-Loop

The system assists product research rather than replacing product judgment.

It should surface evidence, quantify patterns, and highlight opportunities while leaving prioritization decisions to humans.

---

### Principle 5 — Modularity

Data ingestion, preprocessing, AI analysis, clustering, storage, and visualization must remain loosely coupled.

Each component should be independently replaceable without affecting the overall architecture.

---

### Principle 6 — Deterministic Where Possible

Use deterministic software engineering practices wherever practical.

Reserve probabilistic AI reasoning for tasks that genuinely require semantic understanding, such as intent extraction, root cause inference, clustering, and Jobs To Be Done analysis.

# 7. Scope

## In Scope

The system will:

* Collect customer feedback from multiple public sources.
* Normalize and clean textual data.
* Analyze individual reviews using an LLM.
* Generate structured metadata for every review.
* Cluster semantically related feedback.
* Identify recurring product problems.
* Infer Jobs To Be Done.
* Detect user segments.
* Analyze listening behaviors.
* Discover unmet needs.
* Generate opportunity recommendations.
* Produce dashboards and structured reports.
* Allow Product Managers to query insights using natural language.

---

## Out of Scope

The system will not:

* Modify Spotify recommendation algorithms.
* Perform A/B testing.
* Build production recommendation models.
* Predict music preferences.
* Recommend songs directly to users.
* Replace human product strategy.

The objective is insight generation, not recommendation delivery.

---

# 8. Definition of Success

This project is considered successful when a Product Manager can ask questions such as:

> Why do users repeatedly listen to the same content?

or

> Which user segments struggle most with discovering niche artists?

and receive evidence-backed, structured answers generated from thousands of customer conversations within minutes rather than days.

The system should function as an internal AI research assistant rather than a review summarization tool.

This document serves as the implementation contract for Claude Code. All subsequent engineering decisions should align with the business objectives, guiding principles, and architectural intent defined above.
